Our Mission

Creative Placemakers for Bristol

Artspace Lifespace are creative placemakers, curating and caring for a diverse range of spaces to create welcoming places that reflect the community's identity and values — where artists can create, collaborate and present their work.

Our organisation exists to promote art for the benefit of the public by establishing and maintaining facilities for artists from a variety of artistic practices to create, collaborate and present their work.

  • Provide access to affordable, inclusive creative spaces
  • Support artists to create, collaborate and present their work
  • Transform underused buildings into vibrant cultural places
  • Strengthen communities through creativity and culture
Our History

A Journey Through Time and Artspace

Since 2006, Artspace Lifespace has breathed life into over a dozen forgotten buildings across Bristol — from police stations and mansions to department stores and cathedrals.

Our illustrated map traces every space we've curated: from Nine Tree Hill in 2006 to The Island, Arts Mansion, Sparks Bristol and beyond. Each site tells a story of community, creativity and transformation.

We have over 20 years of experience in the 'Meanwhile space' sector — revitalising neglected buildings so they benefit artists, communities and neighbourhoods.

Accessibility

Inclusive by Design

ASLS is committed to inclusivity across all our venues. Sparks Bristol is fully step-free with lift access, a quiet sensory pod, and a prayer room. Please let us know your specific needs when making an enquiry and we'll do everything we can to accommodate.
